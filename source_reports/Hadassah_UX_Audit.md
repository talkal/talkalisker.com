# 01. /summary
We have completed a comprehensive review of the Hadassah Shoulder Unit digital front-door. The primary goal was to simplify the patient journey from symptom discovery to appointment booking. The current site is information-dense but navigationally opaque for elderly patients—the primary demographic.

![Analysis of the Hadassah Homepage Desktop Experience](hadassah_home.png)

# 02. /findings
The current digital experience acts as a library rather than a clinic. Patients are required to read through institutional history before finding actionable symptom guides.

- **Finding:** Information Overload on Homepage | **Severity:** High
  The homepage contains 14 different CTAs, leading to decision paralysis. Patients reported frustration when trying to locate the "Book Appointment" button. We recommend reducing the primary actions to 3 clear paths.

- **Finding:** Mobile Responsiveness on Booking Form | **Severity:** Medium
  Sidebars and popups are overlapping content on small screens (iPhone 13/14). This prevents patients from completing the symptom navigator on the go.

- **Finding:** Visual Contrast | **Severity:** Low
  The light-gray text on white backgrounds in the "Preparation for Surgery" section does not meet accessibility standards for visually impaired patients.

# 03. /strategy
- **Path 1:** Implement a "Symptom Navigator" as the primary hero action.
- **Path 2:** Simplify the menu architecture to "Learn", "Prepare", and "Contact".
- **Path 3:** Redesign the booking form to be a multi-step, localized experience.
